Good to know

Questions, answered.

What does a Summit VA actually do?
Anything repetitive and remote that is pulling you away from leading. That spans inbox and calendar management, customer service and CRM, operations and systems, project and team management, financial tracking and invoicing, lead generation, prospecting, and SOP documentation. We start with the work eating most of your week.
Who do you work best with?
Business owners drowning in operations, music artists and creators who need releases shipped, coaches and consultants who need client delivery systems, and real estate agents who need lead nurture and follow-up. If you are stuck doing everything yourself, you are exactly who we built this for.
How do we get started?
Take the short 2-minute qualifier so we can prepare, then book a free strategy discovery call. On the call we map where your time is going and where support makes the biggest difference. You leave with a clear picture of what working together looks like.
Do you document our processes?
Yes. As we take work on, we write it down as clear SOPs and a systems map. Your business stops living only in your head, which makes you easier to scale and far less dependent on any one person.
I have had a bad VA experience before. How is this different?
Most VA relationships break down because you end up managing the assistant. Summit VA brings executive-level thinking and real systems, so work gets handled proactively, communication stays clear, and you are not retraining someone every week.
What does it cost?
Support is tailored to what your business actually needs, so pricing is set after your strategy call. The qualifier asks about your budget range so the conversation is grounded and useful from the first minute.
